Recommended Path

Build PICKS as a hybrid architecture around governance, integration, and adoption.

PICKS should not duplicate future ERP or MES functionality. It should connect lab workflows, legacy data, governance controls, and enterprise records so GTRI can improve traceability without forcing a single disruptive replacement.

Recommended Path

Integrate instead of replacing everything.

Keep useful existing systems, add a governance and RBAC layer, modernize key workflows, and connect to future ERP and MES choices as they mature.

Design Priorities

Security and traceability lead the design.

The SEMP ranks security, traceability, availability, performance, and adaptability as the quality attributes that drive the architecture.

Future Pathway

Move forward in phases.

Start with MVP scoping, then plan ERP/MES integration, validate usability and pilot workflows, and expand across labs as requirements mature.

Why This Path

It matches the rescope and the final architecture evidence.

USG ERP dependency

The slide deck shows that future ERP decisions affect PICKS. The architecture should stay flexible enough to connect with Oracle, Workday, and approved enterprise systems without recreating them.

Lab adoption risk

Interviews showed fragmented workflows, manual transfer, and legacy usability problems. A phased hybrid approach lets GTRI improve high-value workflows without forcing every lab to change at once.

Compliance readiness

The SEMP emphasizes CMMC, audit logging, access control, and traceability. A governance layer gives those concerns a clear architectural home.

Known Dependencies

Several decisions outside PICKS will shape the implementation path.

USG ERP timeline

The University System of Georgia ERP rollout will affect when PICKS can validate final enterprise interfaces.

Oracle and Workday decisions

Final ERP and finance-system choices will determine exact data fields, integration methods, and synchronization rules.

MES selection

The selected MES direction will shape inventory updates, work-order behavior, lab execution data, and traceability interfaces.

Legacy lab tool transition

PEDYN and other lab tools contain useful history, so migration or adapter planning is needed before broad rollout.
