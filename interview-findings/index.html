Pain Points

What kept showing up

01

Manual workarounds

Users often bridge system gaps with manual entry, emails, spreadsheets, and repeated updates.

02

Disconnected histories

Procurement, inventory, lab execution, and compliance context can be hard to follow as one story.

03

Inconsistent lab practices

Labs use different workflows and customized tools, making a single replacement approach risky.

04

Access control concerns

Broad edit access and unclear ownership can create risk for sensitive or compliance-relevant records.

05

Usability and adoption risk

Legacy interfaces and training burden make adoption as important as technical capability.

Design Implications

How findings shaped PICKS

Integrate before replacing

PICKS should connect useful data and workflows without assuming every lab can immediately abandon existing tools.

Use persistent identifiers

Parts, lots, assets, kits, and builds need identifiers that make history searchable and traceable.

Control protected actions

RBAC and audit logging should be part of the architecture, not an afterthought.

Support adoption

In-system guidance, clear states, and simple workflows reduce the training load for future users.
